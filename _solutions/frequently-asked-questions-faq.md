## How to Apply ◆

> Legacy systems generate repetitive support questions because documentation is outdated, incomplete, or inaccessible. A well-maintained FAQ addresses the most common questions in one easily searchable location.

- Analyze support ticket data and help desk logs to identify the most frequently asked questions. Start with the top twenty questions that account for the majority of support volume.
- Write clear, concise answers that use plain language and include step-by-step instructions with screenshots where the explanation involves navigating the legacy interface.
- Organize FAQs by topic or user role rather than as a flat list. Users should be able to find relevant questions quickly through browsing or searching.
- Make the FAQ searchable with a keyword search that matches questions, answers, and common synonyms for technical terms.
- Establish a maintenance cadence to review and update FAQ entries when the system changes. Outdated answers that describe behavior from a previous version are worse than no FAQ at all.
- Include links from FAQ entries to relevant parts of the application and from the application to relevant FAQ entries, creating a bidirectional connection between help content and the interface.

## Tradeoffs ⇄

> An FAQ provides an efficient self-service channel for common questions but requires ongoing maintenance to remain useful.

**Benefits:**

- Reduces support ticket volume by enabling users to find answers independently for the most common questions.
- Provides consistent answers to frequently asked questions, eliminating the variation that occurs when different support agents explain the same concept differently.
- Serves as a living document of institutional knowledge about how the legacy system works and why certain behaviors exist.
- Accelerates onboarding for new users and new team members who can consult the FAQ before asking colleagues.

**Costs and Risks:**

- An unmaintained FAQ with outdated answers erodes user trust and causes confusion rather than reducing it.
- Writing clear, accurate FAQ entries requires collaboration between technical staff who understand the system and writers who can communicate clearly.
- Users may rely exclusively on the FAQ and not report genuine bugs if their question is tangentially addressed by an existing entry.
- A poorly organized FAQ with hundreds of entries becomes as difficult to navigate as the system itself.

## How It Could Be

> The same questions are asked repeatedly about legacy systems because the answers live only in the heads of experienced users and developers.

A legacy payroll system generates a consistent volume of support calls every pay period because users cannot remember the correct sequence of steps for the period close process. The same twelve questions account for over sixty percent of payroll-related support tickets. The team creates a searchable FAQ with detailed answers for these twelve questions, including annotated screenshots of each step. The FAQ is accessible from within the payroll module through a help menu item and is also linked from the company intranet. Within two pay periods, support ticket volume for routine payroll questions drops noticeably, and the support team can focus on genuine issues rather than repeating the same instructions.
